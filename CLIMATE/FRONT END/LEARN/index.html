Stage 1 • What is Climate Change?

Plain Explanation

Climate change refers to long-term shifts in average temperatures and weather patterns. While natural cycles exist, human activities—especially burning fossil fuels, deforestation, and certain agricultural practices—have intensified greenhouse gas concentrations in the atmosphere, leading to warming and knock-on effects like sea-level rise, extreme weather, and ecosystem stress.

“According to the United Nations” — in simple words

The UN frames climate change as a global challenge requiring urgent, coordinated action to reduce emissions, build resilience, and support adaptation—especially for vulnerable communities. It is central to SDG 13: Climate Action and is connected to all 17 SDGs.

Expand more context

Key international milestones include the 1992 UNFCCC (global framework for cooperation), the 1997 Kyoto Protocol (binding targets for some countries), and the 2015 Paris Agreement (global pact to limit warming well below 2°C and pursue 1.5°C). Implementation involves mitigation, adaptation, and climate finance.
