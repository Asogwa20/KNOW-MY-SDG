Definition and origin

The Sustainable Development Goals were adopted by the United Nations in 2015 as a shared global call to action. They aim to end poverty, protect the planet, reduce inequality, and build peaceful, fair, and resilient societies by 2030. The SDGs replaced the Millennium Development Goals and expanded the agenda into 17 connected goals that cover social, economic, environmental, and institutional progress.

Why the SDGs matter

  • The goals are connected, so progress in health, education, water, energy, and climate often supports each other.
  • They help students understand how local issues connect to global challenges.
  • They turn awareness into action by asking people not only to know problems, but also to respond to them.
